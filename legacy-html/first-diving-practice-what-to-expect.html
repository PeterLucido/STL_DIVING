First Diving Practice: What to Expect

A first practice should feel organized, safe, encouraging, and focused on basic comfort around the board.

All articlesSign Up Instructions

A first diving practice is usually about comfort, safety, and fundamentals. New athletes may not start with flips. They often begin with pool rules, board awareness, entries, body shapes, and simple jumps.

Coaches want to see how the athlete listens, moves, handles height, and responds to feedback. This helps place the diver in the right training lane.

Parents can help by bringing a suit, towel, water bottle, and a patient mindset. Diving rewards repetition, and progress may come in small steps at first.

The first goal is simple: make the diver comfortable, safe, and excited to come back.
