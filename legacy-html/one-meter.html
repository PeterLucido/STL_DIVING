1-Meter Springboard

A core springboard training environment.

1-meter is a core training environment for approach, hurdle, takeoff, body control, and clean entries. It is important for Missouri high school diving, but it is also one part of STL Diving’s broader coach-led training plan.

Why 1-meter matters

1-meter matters because it teaches many of the skills divers use across the sport: approach, hurdle, takeoff, body position, spotting, and clean entries. It is also the board used for Missouri high school competition.

Training starts with entries, lineups, hurdle timing, board control, and body position. From there, divers build voluntary dives, optional dives, meet-list confidence, and the ability to repeat skills under pressure.

Who this is for

New divers, middle school athletes preparing for high school, current Missouri high school divers, swimmers or gymnasts crossing into diving, and returning divers who want cleaner mechanics.

What divers work on

  • Missouri high school 1-meter meet-list development.
  • Approach, hurdle, takeoff timing, and board control.
  • Forward, back, inward, reverse, and twisting progressions as appropriate.
  • Clean entries, body alignment, flexibility, core strength, and dryland drills.
  • Confidence for school tryouts, dual meets, championship season, and off-season improvement.

Progression goal

Divers leave with stronger 1-meter fundamentals, cleaner repeatable mechanics, and a practical path toward high school competition readiness.
